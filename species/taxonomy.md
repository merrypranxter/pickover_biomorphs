# Species Taxonomy

## _base_insectoid
- **Function**: `z = z^z + c`
- **Signature c**: (0.5, 0.1)
- **Morphology**: Arthropod — segmented body plan, radial appendages, chitinous texture
- **Habitat**: Darkfield, transmitted light
- **Scale**: Macroscopic (1-5mm)

## _embryonic
- **Function**: `z = sin(z) + c`
- **Signature c**: (0.3, 0.4)
- **Morphology**: Blastula/gastrula — spherical, translucent, yolk-dense core
- **Habitat**: Phase contrast, saline medium
- **Scale**: Microscopic (50-200µm)

## _radiolarian
- **Function**: `z = z * sin(z) + c`
- **Signature c**: (0.2, 0.15)
- **Morphology**: Heliozoan — radial spines, silicate lattice, hexaradial symmetry
- **Habitat**: Oil immersion darkfield
- **Scale**: Microscopic (20-100µm)

## _hybrid_mutation
- **Function**: `z = z^z + z^c`
- **Signature c**: (0.4, 0.25)
- **Morphology**: Unknown — no existing classification. Structurally between arthropod and deep-sea organism; bioluminescent; resists standard staining protocols
- **Habitat**: Absolute darkfield; no substrate identified
- **Scale**: Unknown (no reference organism)

## _spirochete
- **Function**: `z = z^3 + c`
- **Signature c**: (-0.4, 0.6)
- **Morphology**: Spirochete — helical filamentous bacterium, gram-negative outer membrane, motile via flagellar rotation
- **Habitat**: Phase contrast, saline medium
- **Scale**: Bacterial (0.2–0.5µm diameter, 5–250µm length)
- **Stain**: Gram-negative (safranin counterstain — pink-red); nucleoid deep blue-grey
- **Notes**: The cubic generator produces three-fold tendril symmetry, analogous to the three primary helical pitch directions in spirochete flagella. The helical banding in the rendered form mirrors the structural repeat of the hooked periplasmic flagella that drive spirochete motility.

## _diatom
- **Function**: `z = cos(z) + c`
- **Signature c**: (0.7, 0.2)
- **Morphology**: Pennate diatom — bilateral symmetry, siliceous frustule with raphe slit and transverse striae
- **Habitat**: Brightfield, glass substrate, transmitted white light
- **Scale**: Microscopic (10–200µm in length, depending on genus)
- **Stain**: Unstained; silica autofluorescence; chloroplast gold-brown (chrysophyte chlorophyll a/c + fucoxanthin)
- **Notes**: `cos(z)` is an even function — `cos(-z) = cos(z)` — which directly encodes bilateral symmetry into the biomorph. The striae (periodic ribs) are produced by the `final_arg` modulation at high angular frequency. Reference taxa: Pinnularia, Navicula, Nitzschia.

## _ctenophore
- **Function**: `z = exp(z) + c`
- **Signature c**: (-1.5, 0.1)
- **Morphology**: Ctenophore (comb jellyfish) — gelatinous mesoglea, eight meridional comb rows bearing iridescent ctene plates, aboral sense organ
- **Habitat**: Darkfield, deep ocean aquatic medium
- **Scale**: Macroscopic (1mm–1.5m; rendered at individual-organism scale)
- **Stain**: No chemical stain; bioluminescent and iridescent — light is physical, not chemical
- **Notes**: `exp(z)` produces strong angular periodicity from the imaginary component oscillation. The characteristic 8-fold comb-row symmetry is generated by `sin(final_arg * 4.0)` — four full cycles = eight rows around the meridian. Reference taxa: Mnemiopsis, Pleurobrachia, Beroe.

## _spore
- **Function**: `z = z^2 + c` — Pickover's original iteration
- **Bailout**: Pickover's authentic axis condition: escape when `|Re(z)| > 10 AND |Im(z)| > 10`
- **Mode**: Mandelbrot (parameter plane): `z₀ = 0`, `c = pixel`
- **Morphology**: Fungal ascospore / bacterial endospore — multiple concentric dormancy layers (episporium, exosporium, cortex), PAS-positive polysaccharide wall
- **Habitat**: Brightfield, oil immersion, high magnification — structural cross-section
- **Scale**: Microscopic (1–10µm for bacterial endospores; 5–50µm for fungal ascospores)
- **Stain**: Periodic acid-Schiff (PAS) — polysaccharide-rich wall stains magenta; core: hematoxylin (deep blue-grey)
- **Notes**: This is the only shader that implements Pickover's **exact original algorithm** from *Computers, Pattern, Chaos and Beauty* (1990). The parameter plane view shows the complete biomorph atlas — every point is a different potential organism. The axis-aligned escape condition creates the filamentous axial "stalks" that Pickover described as giving the forms their biological appearance.

## Hybrid Rules
Cross two species by interpolating their:
1. Function components (if compatible)
2. c values (linear or complex interpolation)
3. Iteration parameters (blend thresholds)
4. Stain palettes (overlay or multiply)

Example hybrid: _insectoid_embryonic = z^z + sin(z) + c, c = lerp((0.5, 0.1), (0.3, 0.4), 0.5)
